Best Sword Enchants

a short blog post on the best enchantments for swords in Minecraft.

Published on: January 19, 2026

If you didn't know, Minecraft offers a variety of enchantments for swords. These enchantments can significantly enhance your combat abilities and make battles easier. Obviously, these are completely optional, but they do make a huge difference + it's really fun to gamble your life away to enchantments. Regardless of such, this guide will give you a list of the best sword enchantments to consider! This list is not by order.

1. Sharpness V

Sharpness is the most common and useful enchantment for swords. It's a best all-around damage enchant. It increases melee damage against ALL mobs and players. Personally, this would be the BEST enchantment choice for survival and general PvP.

2. Unbreaking III

This is very controversial in Minecraft forums, but personally, Unbreaking III is a really good enchantment to keep the longevity of your sword. If you don't want to constantly repair or replace your sword, this is the enchantment for you. It increases the durability of your sword, making it last longer before breaking.

3. Mending

Mending is arguable the most POWERFUL and EFFICIENT enchantment in Minecraft. It literally is the best of all time. This is also the exact reason why Unbreaking is said to be completely useless if you have Mending. In short, Mending basically uses in-game experience (XP) to automatically repair your sword when you gain XP.

4. Looting III

Looting is a very useful enchantment that increases drops from mobs. Basically, it increases the number of dropped items. It is really great for farming rare drops from mobs. In general, it's great for survival and people who love to grind.

5. Fire Aspect II

Now hear me out on this one..literally setting your enemies on fire. It is incredibly powerful as it deals extra damage over time. It's great for clearing a whole bunch of mobs fast. However, it can burn up drops, so be cautious when using it for farming.

6. Knockback II

This sounds even weirder. You literally push or knock your enemies away. It's great for controlling crowds, and it helps prevent getting overwhelmed. Generally speaking, it is not great for PvP as it gives your opponent more distance to heal or retaliate.

Final Sayings

Obviously, we believe that you shouldn't be limited by the list above. This is merely based on personal experience and preferences. You can 100% mix and match your own combination of enchantments based on your playstyle and needs.
