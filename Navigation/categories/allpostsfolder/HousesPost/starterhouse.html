Starter Home

Beginner

❌ No 3D Model

Very easy to build cozy starter home! This is a step-by-step process on the EXTERNAL part of the home.

Materials Needed

  • Stripped Birch Log × 16

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Foundation

First, make sure to add the foundation as follow. In this instance, a combination of stone and stone bricks are used to add dimension and design.

Step 2: Fill In

Using spruce planks, make sure to fill in the majority of the build. As shown in the image, one straight line is empty. Instead of filling that with spruce planks, use a furnaces to maximize the space.

Step 3: Pillas

Using spruce logs, build pillars on the corners. In the image, scaffolding is added on top of the spruce logs. You may dirt, or any other block as a placeholder.

Step 4: Connect

Now, connect the pillars as seen in the image. This is why the block on top of the initial spruce logs are important.

Step 5: Fill in Front Side

For this part, use oak planks and sandstone. First layer is sandstone, on top of that, add the planks.
As for the trapdoor area, do it from the inside. Add a placeholder block first and then add the trapdoor form there. It should look like the image as shown.

Step 5: Do this on all sides.

The first image shows what it looks like from the side. This time, the trapdoors should be placed from the outside.
The second image shows the backside of the build. Same process. Again, put the trapdoors from the outside.

Step 5: Side perspective

Here, this is what it should look like on the connecting portion of the home. Do the same process. Make sure to add the trapdoors from the outside.

Step 6: Add Planks - Front & Back

The first image shows what it looks like from the front. Make sure to add a placeholder block. We will be adding a window later on.
The second image shows the back portion of the build. For this part, it should fairly be the same, except you add the trapdoor from the inside now.

Step 6: Add Planks - Side

The first image shows what it looks like from the side. This is away from the continuation of the house itself.
The second image shows what it looks like from the side. This is the nearest side to the continuation of the house.

Step 7: Start of Roof

The image shows how you should do the roof. Use spruce stairs and slab. The log being used it the stripped spruce log.

Step 7: Start of Roof - Other Half

The image shows the other half of the roof. Make sure that both sides are equal and symmtrical as is.
Please take note that you are to do this same structure on ALL sides of the home.

Step 8: Roof Structure

Follow the image as follows. This is basically the structure of the roof. Use slabs for this one.

Step : Fill in Roof

Now, we fill in the gaps on the roof! Honestly, you do not have to follow it exactly if you want. You may fill it in with different blocks.

Step 10: Start of Decorations

On the first image, it shows the front portion of the decorations. Add the door, and add this small doorway. To exitinguish the fire camp, just simply use a shovel to get rid of the fire. Follow the image as follows.
As for the second image, add decorations as follow. Again, to exitinguish the fire from the fireplace, just use a shovel very easily to get rid of.

Step 10: Start of Decoration: Side

The image shows the design as follows. The side design is much more simpler and easy to follow.
Please take note that you are to do this same structure on ALL sides of the home.

Step 11: Porch Side

Place 4 cobblestone wall on the four corners. On top, make sure to add two fences as such. Use spruce gate for the remaining surrounding area.

Step 12: Roof

Now, use fire camps, spruce trapdoors, and leaves to make the roof as follows. Again, make sure to extinguish the fire using a shovel. Otherwise, your build will literally be ashes when you come back.

Step 13: Little Farm

If you have spare time, you may do a formal farm. Otherwise, follow the image as such for a more decoration farm.

DONE
